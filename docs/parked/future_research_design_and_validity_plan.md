# Future Research Design And Validity Plan

Status: parked research design memory.

## Research Identity

Lyra is currently an instrument candidate, not a validated instrument.

Closest external families:

- time-use diary;
- experience sampling / EMA;
- personal informatics;
- interruption/resumption instrumentation;
- planning-fallacy/time-estimation research;
- future JITAI/MRT substrate.

## Candidate Research Questions

1. Do accepted plans predict execution better after personal history is
   accumulated?
2. Does metacognitive discrepancy predict execution drift or missed-plan risk?
3. Do open-thread/re-entry surfaces improve resolution outcomes without shame
   or churn?
4. Does pressure-map visualization improve plan repair or only produce insight?
5. Does AI cold-start estimation help planning without contaminating user
   calibration?
6. Does provider-blind obligation normalization improve capture completeness?

## Required Validity Gates

Every research claim must name:

- population;
- clean-data profile;
- exposure state;
- source provenance;
- missingness handling;
- user burden;
- confound controls;
- falsification criteria;
- kill criteria;
- product contamination risk.

## Minimum Comparison Families

Depending on the claim:

- matched non-switch sessions;
- pre-exposure vs post-exposure windows;
- user-entered estimates vs system-suggested estimates;
- explicit timer sessions vs retroactive corrections;
- provider-import context vs native confirmed tasks;
- personal history vs archetype/research priors;
- open-thread recovery exposure vs no exposure.

## Sample Threshold Posture

Trusted-user traces can generate hypotheses. They cannot validate broad
claims.

Before paper-like claims:

- enough non-operator users for between-user stability;
- enough sessions per user for within-user signal;
- pre-registered exclusions;
- null-result preservation;
- exposure logging;
- no operator-only overfit.
